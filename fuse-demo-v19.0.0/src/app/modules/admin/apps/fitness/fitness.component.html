Understanding Your BMI Result

A BMI range of 18.5 to 24.9 indicates a 'healthy weight.' Maintaining a healthy weight reduces the risk of obesity and type 2 diabetes. Aim for a balanced diet low in fat and sugar, and include plenty of fruits and vegetables. Also, aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise five days a week.

Healthy Eating

Healthy eating supports weight management, disease prevention, digestion, immunity, mental clarity, and mood.

Regular Exercise

Exercise improves fitness, aids weight control, boosts mood, reduces disease risk, and promotes wellness and longevity.

Adequate Sleep

Sufficient sleep enhances mental clarity, emotional stability, physical well-being, and overall rejuvenation.

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is a useful indicator of healthy weight for many, it may not be suitable for everyone. Certain groups should interpret their BMI results with caution, as it may not accurately reflect their health status.

Gender

BMI interpretation considers the developmental and body fat differences between males and females.

Age

In older individuals, BMI may underestimate body fat due to age-related changes in muscle mass.

Muscle

Muscular individuals may be classified as overweight or obese by BMI, as it does not distinguish between muscle and fat.

Pregnancy

Pregnant women naturally gain weight due to fetal growth. Maintaining a healthy BMI before pregnancy is recommended for maternal and fetal health.

Race

BMI may not accurately reflect health risks for individuals of certain racial or ethnic backgrounds. Consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice.
